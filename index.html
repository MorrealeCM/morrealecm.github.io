Step-by-step improvement pathway

Step 1: Translate feedback into 1-2 focus areas Start here

Group similar comments together and choose one high-impact change. Aim for something you can share with students in the first week.

  • Highlight repeated themes (clarity, pacing, assessment).
  • Identify one quick win and one longer-term improvement.
  • Draft a short statement on what will change next term.
Step 2: Co-design solutions with students Collaborate

Invite students to offer ideas. This builds trust and often surfaces small adjustments that make the biggest difference.

  • Share a 5-minute survey during the first week.
  • Offer 2-3 options and let students vote.
  • Communicate what will be piloted and why.
Step 3: Implement with structured support Deliver

Tie your action to weekly routines. Use quick check-ins, rubric refreshers, or open-lab sessions to reinforce change.

  • Add a short agenda slide to every class.
  • Use mid-term feedback to confirm progress.
  • Track outcomes in a simple teaching journal.
Step 4: Reflect and report back Close the loop

Share what changed and what you learned. Closing the loop improves response rates and trust in future evaluations.

  • Provide a short end-of-term update to students.
  • Save evidence of what improved for annual reviews.
  • Celebrate successes and note the next focus area.
